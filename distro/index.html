Station Music

“I pull ma hat down, over ma eyes and let commuters movements lull me to rest. Head against bed of brick. The sound hums beneath ma lids as I give in to its hymn and slip.”
From the musical mind of a man living outside a train station, people are spied on, lives are dreamt of, and memories are recalled and rewritten. Written in a mix of poetry and poetic prose, Station Music provides a closer look at the routes we travel every day and encourages us to look a little closer at what has begun to blur into the background.

On Boundaries

Our daily lives, and even our identities, have become governed by boundaries and labels. What we can and can’t do, what we can and can’t comment on is decided by the label we are given. The boundaries we draw between these labels are increasingly complicated, they can be dividing and they can be empowering. Each essay in this collection discusses the boundaries that exist in modern society, covering issues such as age, gender, race and beauty.

Nothing is Coming,
The Pixels Are Huge.

Blending colourful game-like visuals with contemporary dark story telling, Theatre 42 presents a version of the future that seems all too close for comfort. Nothing is Coming, The Pixels are Huge questions the dark side of technology’s role in society. Using two projectors and 200 cardboard boxes, four actors construct and play out fragments of 1,000 different lives. Rummaging through the building blocks of their existence, they recall and recreate them with the only material at their disposal – pixels. The characters wade through their memories, forget who they are and remember what it is to be human.
Developed at Lancaster University, supported by LICA at Lancaster University, Making Room and Lancaster Arts
